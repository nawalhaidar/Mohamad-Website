About

Welcome! I am Mohamad Fares El Hajj Chehade, a Ph.D. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at The University of Texas at Austin, where I work under the guidance of Dr. Hao Zhu. My research focuses on Risk-aware Transfer in Reinforcement Learning and Physics-aware Supervised Learning, with applications in power and energy systems.

I hold a B.Eng. in Electrical and Computer Engineering with a minor in Mathematics from the American University of Beirut (AUB), where I worked with Dr. Rabih Jabr and Dr. Sami Karaki. In addition, I have interned at the Los Alamos National Laboratory, where I worked with Dr. Wenting Li, Dr. Brian Bell, and Dr. Russell Bent.

Outside academia, I enjoy playing and watching soccer, and I'm an avid participant in related games like Fantasy Premier League and FIFA.

You can connect with me on LinkedIn, view my research on Google Scholar, or explore my projects on GitHub.
