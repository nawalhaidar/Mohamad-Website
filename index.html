About

Welcome! I am Mohamad Fares El Hajj Chehade, a Ph.D. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at The University of Texas at Austin, where I work under the guidance of Dr. Hao Zhu. My research focuses on Risk-aware Transfer in Reinforcement Learning and Physics-aware Supervised Learning, with applications in power and energy systems.

I hold a B.Eng. in Electrical and Computer Engineering with a minor in Mathematics from the American University of Beirut (AUB). During my undergraduate studies, I developed a strong foundation in power and energy systems, culminating in my Final Year Project on Optimal Power Flow via Machine Learning, supervised by Dr. Rabih Jabr, and my Research Project on Microgrid Sizing using Ordinal Optimization, supervised by Dr. Sami Karaki.

My work spans neural network verification, fault diagnosis for power inverters, and the development of sustainable energy solutions.
